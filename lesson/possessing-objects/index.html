The word nina is used to indicate "I have". In a practical sentence, Mimi could be omitted, as nina implies that you are talking about yourself.

Practice the five vocabulary words below for common classroom objects.

I have .
Mimi nina .

The word una means "you have".

You have a book.
Wewe una .

The word ana means "he/she has".

My friend has a notebook.
ana .

The word tuna means "we have".

We have a paper.
Sisi .

The word mna means "you all have".

Do you all have a ruler?
Nyinyi ?

The word wana means "they have". Notice the plural form for "pens" (kalamu) is the same as the singular form.

Do they have pens?
Wao kalamu?

Some words change in the plural, like kitabu (book). When referring to more than one book, use vitabu.

They have the books.
vitabu.

Certain words are pluralized differently based on their noun class, a concept that will be covered in the next unit. For now, you will have to practice to remember the plural of daftari is madaftari.

You have notebooks.
Wewe madaftari .

How would you say this? Use mimi and wewe in your answer.

I have the pen and you have the paper.
