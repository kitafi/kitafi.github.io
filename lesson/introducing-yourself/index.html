This is how you would refer to yourself.

I
Mimi

When describing yourself in the present tense, use ni.

I am
Mimi ni

Type your name into the blank. This is one way to tell someone your name.

I am .
Mimi ni .

You can also use mimi ni to state your profession. Select a profession from the dropdown menu to see its translation.

Notice that the words "a" and "an" don't have a Swahili equivalent.

I am .
Mimi ni .

You are nice.

This is the formal way to say "no". You may also hear a Swahili speaker say la instead of hapana.

no
hapana

The word si is the opposite of ni - you often hear this sound when talking about something in the negative.

I am not American.
Mimi si Mmarekani.

How would you tell someone that you are not a doctor, but rather a businessman?

I am not a doctor. I am a businessman.
Mimi si . Mimi ni .
