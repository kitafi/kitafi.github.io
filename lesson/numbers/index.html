Let's get started with 0, 1, 2, 3.

zero one two three
sifuri moja mbili tatu

In Swahili, numbers may be conjugated based on the noun class of whatever they are quantifying. For example, mwanafunzi (student) in noun class 1 adds the m prefix, and wanafunzi (students pl.) in noun class 2 adds the wa prefix.

Notice wili is sometimes used instead of mbili when conjugated.

one student, two students
mwanafunzi mmoja , wanafunzi wawili

How would you conjugate the number three?

three students
wanafunzi tatu

Here are the next three numbers.

four five six
nne tano sita

When quantifying verbs in the first and second noun class (M-WA), the prefix only is attached to four (nne) and five(tano), not six (sita).

four women, five men, six children
wanawake wanne, wanaume watano, watoto sita

Here are the last single digit numbers.

seven eight nine
saba nane tisa

For the M-WA noun class, a prefix is only attached to eight (nane).

seven interpreters, eight farmers, nine cooks
wafasiri saba, wakulima wanane , wapishi tisa
